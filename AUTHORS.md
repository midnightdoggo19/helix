## Acknowledgements

HelixOS draws on a large body of prior work. While the code is original, the architectural patterns and engineering approaches are common knowledge in the OS-development community. Particular credit to:

- **OSDev community** — the wiki at [wiki.osdev.org](https://wiki.osdev.org) is the irreplaceable single source of truth for the gnarlier corners of x86
- **MIT 6.828 / xv6** — for showing what a teachable Unix-like kernel looks like
- **James Molloy's "Roll your own toy UNIX-clone OS"** — the structure of our boot path owes a debt to this tutorial
- **Philipp Oppermann's `blog_os`** — for cross-pollination with the Rust OS-dev community on patterns we ended up adapting
- **Tanenbaum's _Operating Systems: Design and Implementation_** — the textbook that put scheduler design and memory management into clear words

We also thank everyone who reports bugs, suggests improvements, or stars the repo.

## How to be listed here

1. Open a PR that's substantive (a driver, a syscall, a doc rewrite, a non-trivial fix)
2. Add yourself to the table in the same PR
3. Land

Or, if a maintainer adds you on your behalf, that works too.
